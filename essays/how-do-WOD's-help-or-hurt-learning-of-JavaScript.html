How Do WOD's Help or Hurt Learning JavaScript?

17 Jan 2024

Engaging in the study of JavaScript and athletic software engineering through Workout of the Day (WOD) challenges represents a distinctive approach to learning, significantly divergent from traditional methods. The conceptual familiarity, while present, posed a considerable challenge due to the dynamic nature of the WOD setup. Historically, my learning experiences were predominantly rooted in lectures, homework assignments, reading materials, and video tutorials. The introduction of timed assessments, coupled with a stringent focus on accuracy, presented a formidable obstacle, demanding swift comprehension and application of concepts without the luxury of prolonged contemplation.

The structured format of WODs amplifies the stress inherent in these learning exercises, leaving little room for the leisurely consideration of problems. The binary nature of responses—either knowing the answer or not—introduces a level of frustration, particularly for someone accustomed to conceptualizing solutions away from the computer before translating them into code.

Notwithstanding the inherent challenges, I firmly believe in the efficacy and utility of learning through WODs. They immerse learners in real-world problem-solving scenarios, mirroring situations where employers may demand immediate code production for ongoing projects. This immediacy, characteristic of WODs, aligns with the demands of the professional realm, where the luxury of extended contemplation is a rarity.

Furthermore, the pedagogical value of WODs lies in the emphasis on practical application. Unlike conventional teaching methods that often involve homework and tests, WODs simulate real-world projects, aligning with industry practices. This approach mirrors the assessment criteria in professional settings, where one’s worth is evaluated based on tangible contributions to projects.

A notable outcome of learning through WODs is the heightened understanding of the material, driven by the imposed time constraints. Mastery of programming, particularly in adherence to ES6 coding practices like the arrow function, is facilitated through the condensed and focused nature of WODs. This pedagogical approach elucidates the practical applications of the programming language in use—in this case, JavaScript.

For instance, the versatility of JavaScript is vividly demonstrated through WODs, showcasing its applicability in both front-end and back-end development. This versatility eliminates the need for translating programming languages between different layers of the stack. Additionally, the exercises underscore how JavaScript empowers engineers by allowing the use of functions as variables in various contexts. In essence, WODs serve as a valuable conduit for envisioning the strategic deployment of JavaScript within the field of engineering.
