## Benefits of Smart Questions

When asking questions there are many things that need to be considered, especially when asking online. What kind of effort did the person put into trying to figure out the answer, is the question crystal clear as to what they are asking, etc? A great example of how to ask questions online is displayed by Tal Kohavy. When asking their question their title was straight to the point. It displayed what they needed, \"How to pipe a stream to upload a file from node to Firebase Cloud?\" For this question they described the problem and two ways of fixing it. The first way was to load the entire file to memory before starting to upload. The other way, which was more performant, was by using streams. They give screenshots of both ways they are trying to do them. They also state that the first way was completed but since they wanted the second way, they needed help. They specifically asked for what is needed for them to pass the .pipe() stage. Along with that, they do state that they only asked the question after trying to find an example. This example shows essentially how to develop a smart question. First, it is done by doing your research, you must be able to understand what is at least going on and where the mess up is in the coding. Second, they are asking for a specific question to fulfill a specific duty to upload a file from a node to Firebase Cloud by using streams. Third, they gave all the details surrounding their problem. Because they were asking smart questions, the response was given pretty promptly with much information as far as how to fix the issue. Since a smart question was asked, there was a smart reply. 

## Drawbacks of Asking Questions in the not Smart Way

On the flip side, there are many drawbacks to not asking smart questions. If the question proposed makes it seem as though you have not put any thought into the problem the likelihood of the question being ignored is very high. Another issue with not asking smart questions is that it will inevitably waste people's time. This is due to the people who are trying to help you have to ask more questions to get to the root of the problem in your work. The responders are helping people out because they want to and having them probe to figure out not only the issue but the solution as well wastes their time. This time could be spent answering other questions that other people posted. An example of how we should not ask questions online is asked by Kakarot. With the title, \"when I send( name email password )to the database the email syntax is wrong[closed]\", there is nothing in it that suggests where it is being used or what is this for. It gives no insight into what the problem is other than it is some sort of issue with the email database. Upon looking further into the ticket there is a reference to the code in question but not where the issue is. Just that they want to use a certain syntax when registering the email in the database. Because of the lack of research into what could potentially be the problem, most of the answers are more of follow-up questions trying to figure out the root issue. A specific answer was given to change a line from res.json(users[0]) to res.json(users[0].email) but for that answer, they are still trying to understand what the original question was about. 

## Insights

When it comes to asking questions online, you must follow the correct procedure to make sure that you can actually get the answer you are looking for. This can be done through smart questions. Making sure you research what is needed for your coding to work will make things much easier for the people answering as well as in your ability to get your question answered. Another thing to understand is that the more interesting your question is the more likely it is to be reviewed and answered by other people. If you can draw people to your questions there is a greater likelihood of getting them answered. Along with drawing attention to your question, it is also important to accurately describe the problem as well as what was already tried. This can minimize the amount of work needed to fulfill the coding requirements. The final reason why you should be asking smart questions is because this is a part of your identity. The way that you are interacting and talking with people tells them about you. If you are not properly describing the questions or problems you are having, you could eventually build up a reputation for not knowing the content you are trying to use. Because you have this reputation for lacking knowledge or being lazy, people maybe more inclined to not answer you questions since they assumed that you did not put in some work beforehand when you asked the question.
