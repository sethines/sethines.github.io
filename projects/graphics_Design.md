For this school project, I was given the choice of taking pictures anywhere in the state to enter a contest against all the participants in the school. The main captivating thoughts were to highlight the beauty of Hawaii. The only requirement for the portfolio was to have 3 main highlight pictures. Because of the flexibility in the requirements I chose to do pictures at the beach. 

The main focus of the project was to get to different places to take photos which could then be used to enter into the contest. Once I was at the given places I needed to wait until the right time for the right setting before taking the pictures. Typically this would happen during the evening or early mornings, but sometimes the product would look better post-editing when taken during the day. Finding the right picture in raw formatting was only half the battle. Once the picture was suitable for the project, it needed to be edited. Depending on what was needed to be edited, I used either GIMP or Photoshop.

Some of the best experiences that I learned from this project would be staging is everything. The number of times I needed to retake a picture to finally get that one that I liked took ages and having everything prepared beforehand helped quite a bit. Since photography outside only has a suitable time window that works for the pictures, you need to maximize the time you have by getting everything setup before. Another experience from this project is learning to use editing software like GIMP and Photoshop. These greatly enhanced my photos and allowed for them to shine in all their vibrance.
